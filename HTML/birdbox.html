About Bird Box:

"Bird Box" is a 2019 horror film directed by John R. Leonetti, featuring a cast led by Kiernan Shipka, Stanley Tucci, Miranda Otto, and John Corbett. The screenplay, written by Carey and Shane Van Dyke, is an adaptation of Tim Lebbon's 2015 horror novel of the same name. The narrative revolves around a world under attack by creatures known as "vesps" that hunt using sound. Shipka portrays Ally Andrews, a deaf teenager seeking refuge with her family, while a cult exploits her fertility in the midst of the crisis.

The film explores the challenges faced by Ally's family as they navigate a world dominated by vesps and the struggles to survive in silence. Released on Netflix on April 10, 2019, and in theaters in seven countries later that year, the movie grossed $2.3 million. The plot delves into the family's journey to safety, the dangers they encounter, and the cult's attempt to take advantage of the chaos. With elements of horror, survival, and family dynamics, "Bird Box" offers a tense and suspenseful narrative against the backdrop of a post-apocalyptic world overrun by deadly creatures.
