About The Ritual:

"The Ritual" is a British folk horror film that skillfully blends elements of psychological horror, supernatural terror, and ancient folklore. Directed by David Bruckner and based on Adam Nevill's novel, the film follows four friends who embark on a hiking trip in the Swedish wilderness to honor their deceased friend. The narrative takes a sinister turn as they venture into a mysterious forest, encountering unsettling symbols, an abandoned cabin with eerie artifacts, and the malevolent presence of an ancient evil force.

The film's strength lies in its atmospheric tension and psychological horror, gradually building a sense of dread and unease. As the friends face nightmares, inexplicable occurrences, and the horrifying consequences of their decisions, "The Ritual" explores themes of guilt, trauma, and the supernatural. The visual storytelling, supported by strong performances, contributes to the film's immersive and chilling experience. With its gripping plot, atmospheric cinematography, and unexpected twists, "The Ritual" stands out as a compelling addition to the folk horror genre, keeping audiences engaged from start to finish.
