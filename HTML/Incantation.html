About Incantation:

"Incantation" is a 2022 Taiwanese found footage supernatural folk horror film directed by Kevin Ko, co-written by Ko and Chang Che-wei. The film, released in Taiwan on March 18, 2022, gained significant success, becoming the highest-grossing Taiwanese horror film. Netflix provided international distribution starting from July 8, 2022.

The story unfolds through a non-linear found footage narrative, narrated by a woman named Li Ronan. She implores viewers to memorize an insignia and chant an incantation to bless and lift a curse on her daughter, Dodo. Six years prior, Ronan, her boyfriend Dom, and Dom's cousin Yuan trespassed a remote clan village during a ritual, breaking a religious taboo. The clan asked them to submit their names with the incantation to the Mother Buddha, an ancestral deity. Ronan, unaware of her pregnancy, learned she had to submit her daughter's name after birth. "Incantation" blends supernatural horror with folklore, exploring the consequences of breaking religious taboos and the complex nature of curses. The non-linear storytelling adds suspense, and the unexpected twist challenges traditional horror tropes, making it a notable entry in the genre
