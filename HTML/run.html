About Run:

"Run," directed by Aneesh Chaganty, is a psychological horror thriller that delves into the disturbing relationship between Chloe, a disabled teenager brilliantly portrayed by Kiera Allen, and her seemingly caring but manipulative mother, Diane, played by Sarah Paulson. The film intricately weaves a tale of deception, unveiling a web of lies as Chloe discovers her mother's sinister intentions. The tension escalates as Chloe fights to break free from her mother's control, leading to a climactic confrontation that exposes the extent of Diane's disturbing actions. The film's strong performances, particularly by Kiera Allen, contribute to its success, making it a gripping and emotionally charged exploration of maternal betrayal and resilience.

The conclusion of "Run" is both unsettling and cathartic, as it fast-forwards seven years into the future to reveal Chloe's transformed life and her poignant visit to Diane in a correctional facility. The symbolic use of medication underscores the complex emotions at play, leaving audiences with a lingering sense of the psychological trauma endured by Chloe and the profound impact of her journey toward independence.
