About Re/Member:

"Remember" is a 2022 South Korean action film directed by Lee Il-hyung, featuring Lee Sung-min and Nam Joo-hyuk in lead roles. The movie serves as a remake of the 2015 Canadian film of the same title, with its narrative set against the backdrop of the Korean-Japanese relationship during World War II. Released on October 26, 2022, the film revolves around the character Pil-Joo, an octogenarian Alzheimer's patient who, having lost his entire family during the Japanese colonial era, embarks on a quest for revenge before his memories fade away. The plot also involves a young man in his twenties, portrayed by Nam Joo-hyuk, who aids Pil-Joo in his mission.

The cast includes Lee Sung-min as Han Pil-joo, Nam Joo-hyuk as Park In-gyu, Jung Man-sik, Park Geun-hyung, Song Young-chang, Moon Chang-gil, and Park Byung-ho in supporting roles. The film gained international recognition by being invited to the 42nd Hawaii International Film Festival, and its distribution rights were pre-sold to numerous countries, including France, Russia, India, Hong Kong, Vietnam, Malaysia, Taiwan, Singapore, and North America. "Remember" explores themes of revenge, memory, and the enduring impact of historical events on individual lives.
